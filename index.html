Dominican Order

The Dominican Order/Order of Preachers (Latin: Ordo Praedicatorum; English: Order of Preachers) is a Catholic religious order founded by Saint Dominic de Guzmán. The Order received official approval from Pope Honorius III on December 22, 1216.

Prayer

Prayer is the foundation of Dominican life. Members of the Dominican Order are called to cultivate a deep relationship with God through personal prayer and liturgy, especially in the celebration of the Eucharist and the Divine Office (Liturgia Horarum). Through prayer, Dominicans receive strength to carry out their mission of preaching and service.

Study

Study holds a very important place in Dominican life, as deep knowledge is necessary to effectively proclaim the Gospel. Saint Dominic believed that theological and philosophical studies equip members of the Order to better understand the faith and to respond to the challenges and questions of their time.

Comunity

Community life is at the heart of the Dominican calling. They live together in fraternity, supporting one another and sharing a life of faith. Life in community helps Dominicans build character, grow in fraternal love, and deepen their commitment to service and mission.

Preaching

Preaching is the main purpose of the Dominican Order, known as the “Order of Preachers.” Dominicans are sent to proclaim the Good News through both words and example, especially to those in need of comfort and truth, with a spirit of compassion and truth.
